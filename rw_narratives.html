Convenience / Strip
Curbline Properties (CURB) was the standout this week with a +2.0% gain, continuing its strong June momentum (up +7.8% for the month) following a successful $400M at-the-market equity offering and Q1 results that impressed on occupancy and leasing velocity in necessity-driven convenience corridors. Whitestone REIT (WSR) effectively traded in a tight range near its $19.00 acquisition price following Ares Management's all-cash takeover announcement in April, making it a merger-arb rather than operational story. INRE, as an OTC non-traded REIT, had limited price discovery this week.
Regency Read-Through: Curbline's strong access to capital and premium pricing for convenience-format assets validates the segment's institutional appeal; Regency's own anchor-driven neighborhood centers benefit from similar demand dynamics without the execution risk of CURB's lighter-format model.
Grocery-Anchored
InvenTrust (IVT) touched a fresh 52-week high of $36.15 on June 17, and Regency Centers (REG) advanced ~+1.0% on continued institutional buying following strong REITweek reception and raised 2026 guidance (SS NOI +4.4%). Kite Realty (KRG) was the week's laggard in the group, slipping -1.3% after announcing a capital recycling program of $136M in acquisitions and $255M in dispositions that was initially read as dilutive. ROIC continued to trade tight to its Blackstone deal price, removing it from price-discovery consideration. Grocery-anchored implied cap rates firm near 5.5–5.9% for best-in-class operators, well inside the private market's ~6.7% national average.
Regency Read-Through: The grocery-anchored public-private cap rate gap (~110–130 bps) remains a structural advantage for Regency's acquisition pipeline; KRG's capital recycling template — selling non-core assets to fund Sun Belt growth — mirrors Regency's own strategy and confirms sector conviction in high-quality open-air.
Power / Diversified
Agree Realty (ADC) was the week's strongest performer in this segment, gaining +3.3% on continued net lease demand and a dividend hike that further underscored balance sheet health; the stock's implied cap rate compresses toward 5.5%, reflecting best-in-class net lease underwriting. Urstadt Biddle has been fully absorbed into Regency Centers and no longer trades independently, while PREIT lacks meaningful public market data following its restructuring process. The segment's headline is dominated by ADC's outperformance relative to mall and strip peers.
Regency Read-Through: ADC's premium pricing for high-quality, net-leased retail reinforces the valuation discipline Regency applies to its own development-adjacent acquisitions; Regency's superior portfolio quality should support tighter implied caps than most diversified peers.
Mall / Lifestyle
Macerich (MAC) was the week's biggest loser across all 21 issuers, falling -6.4% to $16.88 after recent momentum ran into profit-taking and macro rate sensitivity; despite a solid 30-day and YTD performance (+28.2%), the stock's elevated implied cap (~7.5%) reflects ongoing concerns about anchor tenant mix and refinancing costs. BofA's downgrade of Tanger (SKT) to Underperform on June 16 weighed on the segment even though SKT's Q1 Core FFO grew +11% YoY and its dividend was hiked 7%; three analysts raised targets the same week (BMO to $42, Scotiabank to $41), signaling analyst divergence. Simon Property Group (SPG) pulled back -1.7% from recent highs but remains the segment's sole Healthy-rated operator on balance sheet strength.
Regency Read-Through: Mall segment volatility and cap rate divergence from grocery-anchored (~7.2% vs. 5.9%) continues to highlight the risk-adjusted return advantage of Regency's open-air, grocery-anchored format, particularly as enclosed malls face structural headwinds that Regency's tenant roster avoids.
Net Lease
Essential Properties (EPRT) was the week's best net lease performer (+1.5%), buoyed by its successful $400M notes offering at 5.375% (June 15 closing) and a 3% quarterly dividend increase to $0.32, validating the thesis of its 99.7%-leased, 3.5x covered portfolio. Getty Realty (GTY) also outperformed (+1.1%), having raised full-year AFFO guidance to $2.50–$2.52 per share; its 5.9% yield and 99.7% occupancy make it a steady compounder. STORE Capital (STOR) continued to trade near its acquisition price as ISS and Glass Lewis backed the GIC/Oak Street deal, while Broadstone (BNL) benefited from favorable press around its Tesla build-to-suit pipeline expansion.
Regency Read-Through: Net lease's ongoing M&A wave (STOR, potentially others) compresses competition for high-quality essential retail tenants — a positive for Regency, which competes on grocery-anchored tenancy rather than net lease structures and can absorb migrating tenant demand at market rents.
