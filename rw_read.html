• Grocery-Anchored Remains the Standout Segment. Regency Centers (REG) and InvenTrust (IVT) led peers this week — IVT hit a fresh 52-week high of $36.15 — as 4.4% same-property NOI growth and near-full occupancy underpin investor confidence. For Regency, REITweek commentary reinforcing the $600M+ development pipeline kept buy-side interest elevated even as the broader market softened.
  • Mall REITs Stumbled on Macro Headwinds and a High-Profile Downgrade. Macerich (MAC) fell -6.4% for the week — the steepest decliner in the universe — while Simon Property Group (SPG) slipped ~-1.7% from $214.86 to $211.33. BofA's downgrade of Tanger (SKT) to Underperform on June 16 added sector-wide pressure, though SKT's Core FFO growth of +11% YoY and a 7% dividend raise reflect solid underlying fundamentals.
  • Net Lease Net Positive: Capital Markets Activity Signals Confidence. Essential Properties (EPRT) closed a $400M notes offering at 5.375% on June 15 and raised its quarterly dividend 3% to $0.32, reflecting balance sheet strength in a 99.7%-leased portfolio. Agree Realty (ADC) posted the strongest 7-day return in the Net Lease group at +3.3%, and Getty Realty (GTY) raised full-year AFFO guidance to $2.50–$2.52.
  • M&A Activity Reshaping the Universe. Three issuers are in active deal processes: Whitestone REIT (WSR) agreed to be acquired by Ares for $19.00/share (~12% premium), ROIC was approved for sale to Blackstone, and STORE Capital (STOR) proxy advisors ISS and Glass Lewis both recommended shareholder approval of the GIC/Oak Street acquisition. This wave of takeouts — the most active M&A year for REITs in a decade per Nareit — compresses the available investable universe and signals private capital sees public REIT discounts as an opportunity.
  • Public-Private Cap Rate Gap Narrows but Persists. Grocery-anchored assets trade at a national average private-market cap rate of ~6.7% (JLL YE2025), while REIT-implied cap rates for leading grocery-anchored operators sit ~110-130 bps tighter, keeping the public-private spread above 100 bps. The gap is a continued tailwind for REIT acquisitions and disciplined dispositions. Regency's competitive implied cap rate advantage supports external growth as private buyers pay up for institutional-quality Sunbelt assets.
